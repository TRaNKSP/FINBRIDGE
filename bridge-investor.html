How the Domestic Reserve Works

15% of all BRIDGE Fund capital is held in a domestic reserve and deployed into US communities — creating direct benefit for American investors beyond their financial return:

US Gig Worker Micro-Loans
Advances to Uber, DoorDash, and TaskRabbit workers facing fuel or income gaps — backed by the domestic reserve fund at 0% cost to the worker.
Community Energy Projects
Solar microgrid installations in underserved US communities. Energy savings flow back to residents while generating carbon credits for the fund.
Agricultural Harvest Advances
Seasonal credit for US small farmers — $200 to $10,000 — at below-market rates subsidized by the global fund's overall returns.
CRA Credit for Bank Partners
Bank investors receive qualifying CRA credit for the entire investment — not just the domestic reserve — under the Community Finance Investment Act framework.
